The Watermelon Woman is a landmark of the New Queer Cinema of the 1990s, a time of queer formal experimentation with cinematic language.

Set in the late 17th century, the story follows Benedetta Carlini, a nun in a convent in Italy, who experiences disturbing religious visions and engages in a forbidden lesbian affair with another nun.

Yoon-ju, an art student, bumps into Ji-soo at the junk shop and slowly falls in love with her.
